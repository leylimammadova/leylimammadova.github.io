Leyli Mammadova

Mathematician | KU Leuven

I am a PhD student at KU Leuven, working on moment maps in multisymplectic geometry in the geometry section. I grew up in Baku, Azerbaijan and previously was a master student at ETH Zurich. My interests include differential geometry and mathematical physics, and I enjoy teaching mathematics tutorials to undergraduate students. I am co-organising the junior seminar in pure mathematics at KU Leuven. My other passions are nature and cats: I live in a small appartment, but secretly play with the cats in my street.

Publications and pre-prints

My thesis supervisor is Marco Zambon. I am also working on a publication with Leonid Ryvkin (Paris Diderot).

Interests

I have been fascinated with mathematics since childhood, and became excited about differential geometry and mathematical physics as a student. Topics of interest include multisymplectic geometry and higher Lie theory.
